The Challenge

A grid under pressure

Once DERs arrive without a control layer, four problems show up fast.

1

Lack of visibility

Operators fly blind — they can't see the power these devices generate or consume in real time. A massive data gap.

2

Outage complexity

A solar panel can still energize a disconnected feeder — a safety hazard for linemen and a complication for restoration.

3

Congestion & overloads

Unpredictable, bidirectional flow pushes transformers and lines past their limits.

4

Voltage fluctuation

Intermittent solar drives sudden voltage spikes and sags — instability and potential equipment damage.

What is DERMS?

Distributed Energy Resource Management

The software layer that turns thousands of distributed assets — solar, storage, flexible load — into coordinated, dispatchable grid resources rather than unpredictable variables.

Monitor

Real-time tracking of every DER's performance, status, and grid impact.

Manage

Coordinate dispatch and response across diverse assets and operators.

Optimize

Maximize grid reliability and efficiency against operational objectives.

ADMS runs the wires. DERMS runs the energy resources on them. VSIP 4 needs both.
Why DERMS

Why DERMS — and why now

The distribution grid was never designed for what's now connecting to it.

01

Rising DER penetration

Exponential growth in solar, storage, and EVs is changing grid dynamics.

02

Legacy limitations

Infrastructure built for one-way flow from central generation to loads.

03

Visibility gap

Without DERMS, no real-time awareness of DER behavior and impacts.

04

Stability risks

Unmanaged DERs cause voltage excursions, overloads, protection issues.

Types of DERMS

Four kinds of DERMS

Each is optimized for a different operator, objective, and use case.

Utility / Grid

Grid reliability and distribution operations. VSIP 4's need.

VPP / Aggregated

Market participation and wholesale energy trading.

Customer-Centric

Behind-the-meter optimization and customer value.

Market-Integrated

Coordination between grid operations and markets.

VSIP owns the assets and runs the grid — so the foundation is Utility / Grid DERMS.
